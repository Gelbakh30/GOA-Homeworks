After devastating casualties by each other during World War II, the United States and Japan were able to establish a strong post-war diplomatic alliance. The U.S. State Department still refers to the American-Japanese relationship as "the cornerstone of the United States security interests in Asia and... fundamentally for regional stability and prosperity."
The Pacific half of World War II, which began with the Japanese attack on American naval base Pearl-HarHawaii, on December 7, 1941, ended almost four years after Japan surrendered to the U.S.-led Allies on 2 September 1945. The surrender came after the United States dropped two atomic bombs on Japan. Japan lost 3 million people in the war.
